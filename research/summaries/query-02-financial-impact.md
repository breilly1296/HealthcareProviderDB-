# Query #2: Financial Impact of Provider Directory Inaccuracies

## Source
Haeder, S.F. & Xu, W.Y. (2024). "Consumer Experiences Navigating Health Care Provider Directories." World Medical & Health Policy.

## Key Findings

### Surprise Medical Bills
- **4x higher surprise bills** when directories are inaccurate (16% vs 4%)
- **2x more likely to go out-of-network** unintentionally (40% vs 20%)

### Care Access Impact
- **28% delayed care** due to directory errors
- **10% forgone care entirely** because of directory issues

### Patient Harm
- **51% reported financial problems** stemming from directory inaccuracies
- **54% reported health problems** as a result

## Product Implications

### Marketing Strategy
- Lead with the "4x surprise bills" statistic - concrete, alarming, relatable
- Emphasize dual harm: financial AND health consequences
- Position VerifyMyProvider as solving a documented public health crisis

### Messaging Framework
- **Problem:** Insurance directories are dangerously inaccurate
- **Impact:** Real financial harm (surprise bills) and delayed/forgone care
- **Solution:** Real-time, crowdsourced verification prevents these outcomes

### Target Audience Prioritization
1. **Patients with chronic conditions** - most likely to delay/forgo care
2. **Budget-conscious families** - surprise bills have biggest impact
3. **Out-of-network worriers** - those who've been burned before

## Implementation Notes
- Use these stats prominently on landing page hero section
- Create case studies around avoided surprise bills
- Develop cost calculator: "How much could bad directories cost you?"
